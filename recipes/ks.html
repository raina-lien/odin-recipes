Korv Stroganoff (Swedish Sausage Stroganoff)

Description

Korv Stroganoff, or Swedish sausage stroganoff, is a delicious, easy meal that is perfect for hurried weeknights and picky eaters. It's also relatively inexpensive and easy to throw together with pantry staples. If you're in Sweden, falukorv is the obvious choice, but for those in other countries, hot dogs or un-sliced bologna (or any mild sausage) works fabulously as well. Serve with rice, pasta, or potatoes (or even roasted cauliflower for a low-carb twist), parsley on top, and sliced raw veggies on the side!

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • 400 g falukorv or other sausage
  • 0.5 cup creme fraiche
  • 0.5 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 0.25 cup tomato paste
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 tsp beef stock concentrate
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parsley for garnish
  • Cooked rice, pasta, potatoes, or any other grain/carb to serve the stroganoff atop
  • Raw veggies to eat on the side (cucumber and tomatoes are my favorite!)

Steps

  1. Cut the falukorv (or other sausage) into bite-sized strips.
  2. Dice the onion and fry in olive oil until golden. Set aside.
  3. Fry the falukorv until browned.
  4. Add back in onion and tomato paste, and fry tomato paste for 30 seconds.
  5. Stir in creme fraiche, cream, and stock concentrate.
  6. If the stroganoff is too thick, it can be thinned with water until the desired consistency is reached.
  7. Salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve the stroganoff over rice (or other carb), topped with parsley, and alongside raw vegetables.

Enjoy!
