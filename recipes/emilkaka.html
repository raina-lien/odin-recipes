Description

I was craving buttercream, my husband was craving a light, fluffy cake. My first thought was to make a traditional Swedish chocolate coffee cake known as "KÃ¤rleksmums;" but of course, we were out of cocoa powder. Another option was a delicious "Sylviakaka," which required up to six eggs depending on which recipe one chose; we only had a few eggs and I wanted to save two for breakfast. So, it seemed, I had no choice but to create my own cake. And thus Emilkaka, or Emil's cake- named for my husband who said it was the best cake he's ever had- was born. A fluffy, vanilla-y, super sweet, utterly decadent cake best enjoyed with a cup of coffee. Enjoy!

Ingredients

For the cake:
  • 120 grams white flour (1 cup)
  • 1 tsp. baking powder
  • 0.5 tsp. salt
  • 2 tsp. vanilla extract or 1 tbsp. vanilla sugar
  • 0.75 cup sugar
  • 0.5 cup water or milk
  • 50 grams butter
For the vanilla pudding:
  • 0.5 cup instant vanilla pudding powder
  • Milk (enough to make a thick pudding, exact amount can vary depending on pudding brand)
For the buttercream frosting:
  • 150 grams butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tbsp. vanilla extract or 1.5 tbsp. vanilla sugar
  • 1 egg yellow
